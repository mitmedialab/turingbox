The rapid growth of artificial intelligence has hindered our ability to replicate and audit the behavior of emerging AI systems. These hindrances have opened the door to unintended consequences like unexpected biases, unethical behavior, and unequal access to these systems.

TuringBox is a two-sided marketplace that facilitates both the behavioural study of artificially intelligent systems and the production of new, socially-aware systems.

On one side of the market, examiners can study and utilize the behavior of algorithms by creating boxes, which are tasks paired with datasets that create an observable environment for the algorithm. On the other side creators can upload existing and novel artificially intelligent systems to benchmark their algorithms and solve boxes to gain reputation in our community and the artifical intelligence community as a whole.
